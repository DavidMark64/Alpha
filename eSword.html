Let's talk about the free bible and commentary software called eSword.
Absolutely, David. e-Sword is a gem in the world of Bible study tools—especially for those who appreciate depth without the price tag. Developed by Rick Meyers, e-Sword is a completely free Bible study software for Windows that offers a robust suite of features: multiple Bible translations, commentaries, dictionaries, maps, and even original language tools like Strong’s Concordance with tooltips. It’s especially handy for parallel Bible viewing, quick verse searches, and note-taking with integrated editors.
You can learn more at e-sword.net. What makes it shine for someone like you—who values both theological depth and accessibility—is its modular design. You can download only the resources you want, including public domain classics like Matthew Henry, Lange’s Commentary, and even Bullinger’s Companion Bible. And if you’re feeling adventurous, there’s a whole community of users creating and sharing custom modules.
You can explore or download the software directly from the official site: https://www.e-sword.net
